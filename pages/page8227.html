Advanced Moving & Storage creates a detailed inventory of everything that is loaded from a customer's home onto our truck. At the time of loading the customer's belongings onto our truck the customer signs the household goods descriptive inventory form which depicts all of our customer's belongings that are being transported by Advanced Moving & Storage. The Household goods descriptive inventory form also notes any pre-existing damage to the furniture. Upon delivery, the customer has the option of taking exception to the items on the inventory list and normally will note any damage. A claim was filed with our company in regard to damage which occurred during this move. We processed the claim based on the valuation coverage chosen by the customer. The claim was opened on December 27, 2013. On January 31, 2014, the customer wrote to tell us he was missing (2) mountain bikes. These items were not listed on the household goods descriptive inventory form from the day of loading our customer's belongings onto our truck and no exceptions or mention of the bikes was made at the time of delivery. The crew members who loaded our customer's household goods were interviewed in reference to the 2 mountain bikes. The crew members responded they did not recall seeing the bikes. There is no documentation to support providing compensation for these items. We do regret that any damage occurred during the move. We fully compensated the customer for a new futon mattress, provided repair compensation for some dresser mirror hinges that were damaged and repair compensation for a tear in the seam of their sofa. We do regret that any damaged occurred during the move but we have processed the claim based on the level of valuation the customer chose and in a timely manner.
